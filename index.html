School rules:

We have also previously talked that the school is an educational institution governed by a set of regulations and laws that regulate the mechanism of work with it in order to achieve the advancement of the entire educational process and serve the interests of society, and it is worth noting that both the administration, the higher educational bodies and students must abide by the school’s laws. Below, we will learn about the most important of these laws.

Laws for students:

  1. Maintaining the school's facilities including halls, tools, tools, and others.
  2. Coming to school on the official working hours, and no delay under any circumstances.
  3. Avoid absenteeism from school, which exposes parents to legal prosecution.
  4. Students' commitment to school uniforms.
  5. Participating in activities and avoiding racism or insulting colleagues.
  6. Submitting a medical report when absent due to illness, and requesting prior permission before absence from school in other cases.
  7. Doing homework and not cheating in public exams and regular exams. Dealing with respect and affection with all the school staff, including students, teachers, and workers.
  8. Prevent the use of cell phones in school, and the use of phones is also prohibited to abuse drugs or any kind of drugs and alcohol.
  9. Attending the classes specified by the administration, and not running away from school under any circumstances.
  10. Keeping the school clean and not dirt on the floor.

Laws pertaining to the administrative body and teachers:

  1. Commitment to the specified official working hours.
  2. Giving courses according to the followed teaching plan.
  3. Dealing with students with love and respect.
  4. Not to discriminate between students, and to avoid racist or class dealing.
  5. Reliance on the language of dialogue as a means of communication with students.
  6. Explaining the curricula in an understandable, clear, and simple manner.
  7. Maintain confidentiality of questions of periodic exams and exams.
  8. Not accepting bribery, or mediation under any form.
  9. Taking care of school facilities and carrying out restoration when needed.
  10. Encouraging the development process in the school and participating in it.
